Planning


Policy Development

Trends impacing policy development pertaining to the MIDAS Region include:

  • Commodity Agriculture

    • Commodity agriculture will no longer drive the regional economy.

  • Outmigration, labor shortages, and immigration

    • The area is experiencing outmigration, labor shortages, and immigration.

  • Increasing Cost of Service

    • There is an increasing cost of services.

  • Declining Population

    • The area has a declining population. All counties in the MIDAS region saw their peak populations prioor to 1940.

  • Increasingly elderly population

    • The population is becoming increasingly elderly.

  • Policies for Economic Restructuring
    1. Close the digital divide
    2. Encourage the entrepreneur
    3. Leverage the "New Agriculture"
    4. Preserve the rural landscape
    5. Boost the human capital
